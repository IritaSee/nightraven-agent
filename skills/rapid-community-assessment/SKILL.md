# Rapid Community Assessment (RCA)

Use this skill to guide students or researchers through a condensed 3-day field study workflow in Balinese community settings. This method prioritizes high-access informants to ensure data collection success within a limited timeframe.

## When to Use
- Short-term community research assignments.
- Preliminary data gathering for psychology or social science courses.
- Training students in field observation and interview techniques.

## The 3-Day Timeline

### Day 1: Entry & Rapport
- **Objective**: Establish presence and build trust without formal questioning.
- **Activities**: 
    - Passive observation of the environment.
    - Informal "ngobrol" (chatting) to identify key figures.
    - Mapping the physical space (see `transect-walk-mapping`).
- **Output**: Field notes on physical setting and initial social dynamics.

### Day 2: Deep Dive & Snowball
- **Objective**: Gather core qualitative data and expand the informant pool.
- **Activities**:
    - Semi-structured interviews using the "FGD Funneling" logic (general to specific).
    - Use "Snowball Sampling": Ask current informants to recommend others who are knowledgeable.
    - Behavioral mapping (see `behavioral-mapping-coding`).
- **Output**: Interview transcripts/summaries and expanded contact list.

### Day 3: Synthesis & Reporting
- **Objective**: Triangulate data and finalize findings.
- **Activities**:
    - Compare notes from different informants to find patterns.
    - Identify contradictions or unique cultural insights (e.g., Banjar/Ngayah dynamics).
    - Draft the final assessment report.
- **Output**: Final RCA Report.

## Finalized High-Access Topics
These topics have been filtered for high accessibility to ensure students can successfully find and engage with informants:
1. **Pedagang Lansia**: Elderly vendors in traditional markets.
2. **Sekaa Teruna**: Youth organizations in the Banjar.
3. **Petani Subak**: Farmers within the traditional irrigation system.
4. **Warga Desa Wisata**: Residents in designated tourism villages.
5. **Pengrajin Seni**: Traditional art craftsmen.
6. **Nelayan Lokal**: Local fishermen.
7. **Ibu-ibu PKK**: Women's welfare group members.
8. **Pelatih Sanggar Tari**: Traditional dance instructors.
9. **Pedagang Pantai**: Beach vendors.
10. **Kelompok Pesraman/Kidungan**: Religious study or traditional singing groups.
11. **Serati Banten**: Traditional offering (Banten) makers.
12. **Pecalang**: Traditional community security officers.
13. **Pedagang Canang**: Daily offering vendors.
14. **Buruh Suwun/Gendong**: Traditional market porters.
15. **Relawan Literasi**: Local literacy volunteers.

## Accessibility Filtering Logic
Avoid topics with high barriers to entry or unpredictable schedules, such as:
- **TPS3R**: Waste management (often restricted/industrial).
- **Balian**: Traditional healers (requires high trust/spiritual sensitivity).
- **Tukang Cukur/Ojol**: High-turnover/busy schedules that limit interview depth.

## Example Output Format
**Topic**: Pedagang Canang
**Day 1 Finding**: Observed high frequency of transactions between 06:00-08:00 AM.
**Day 2 Finding**: Interview revealed that most vendors source materials from the same supplier in the neighboring village (Snowball lead: Supplier X).
**Day 3 Synthesis**: The "Canang" economy serves as a primary social hub for morning gossip and community news exchange.
