# Balinese Psychology Design

Use this skill to synthesize Western psychological theories with Balinese cultural concepts for lecture materials, assignments, and research.

## When to Use
- Designing course materials for "Psikologi Lansia", "Psikologi Kognitif", or "Inclusive Education".
- Creating student assignments that require local cultural context.
- Developing research frameworks that involve Balinese populations.

## Concepts to Integrate
- **Ngayah**: Selfless service/volunteering; use when discussing prosocial behavior, community psychology, or motivation.
- **Banjar**: Community-based social structure; use when discussing social support systems, elderly care, or group dynamics.
- **Karma**: Cause and effect; use when discussing coping mechanisms (Lazarus & Folkman), moral development, or belief systems.
- **Menyama Braya**: Concept of brotherhood/togetherness; use when discussing empathy, social cohesion, or conflict management.

## Steps
1. **Identify Core Theory**: Start with a standard psychological model (e.g., PERMA, GROW, or Timothy Clark's 4 Stages).
2. **Map Cultural Analogues**: Identify which Balinese concept aligns with or provides a local lens for the theory.
3. **Synthesize Content**: Create lecture slides or reading materials that explain the theory using the Balinese concept as a case study or explanatory framework.
4. **Bilingual Output**: Ensure the final materials are provided in both Indonesian and English as per user preference.

## Example
**Theory**: Lazarus & Folkman's Coping Theory.
**Integration**: Explain "Emotion-focused coping" through the lens of **Karma Phala**, where acceptance of one's situation is facilitated by cultural beliefs in cosmic justice.
