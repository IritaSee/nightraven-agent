# Transect Walk Mapping

Use this skill to conduct community-based spatial analysis that goes beyond physical structures to uncover social and cultural meanings.

## When to Use
- Community needs assessments.
- Participatory Rural Appraisal (PRA).
- Urban/Rural psychology research.

## Key Concept
A **Transect Walk** is a systematic walk along a defined path (cross-section) through an area, guided by local community members to explain the "unseen" aspects of the environment.

## Steps
1. **Identify Guides**: Select local residents who represent different demographics (e.g., elders, youth, leaders).
2. **Define the Path**: Draw a line on a map that crosses diverse zones (e.g., residential, commercial, sacred, waste).
3. **The Walk**: Walk the path slowly. Ask guides about:
    - **Invisible Boundaries**: "Where does this neighborhood end?"
    - **Social Meanings**: "What happens in this empty lot at night?"
    - **History**: "What used to be here?"
4. **Documentation**: Take photos and notes on the "social layer" of the map.

## Output Format
- **Transect Line**: [Description of path]
- **Guides**: [Demographics/Roles]
- **Zone Analysis**:
    - **Zone A (Physical)**: [Description] -> **(Social)**: [Meaning/Usage]
    - **Zone B (Physical)**: [Description] -> **(Social)**: [Meaning/Usage]

## Example
> **Zone: Village Entrance**
> - **Physical**: Large banyan tree and stone gate.
> - **Social Meaning**: Identified by guides as a "neutral zone" where different Banjar groups meet for informal negotiations; considered sacred after 6 PM.
