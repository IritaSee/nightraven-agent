# Triad Coaching Framework

Use this framework to facilitate coaching practice sessions that prioritize active listening and peer feedback over direct advice-giving.

## When to Use
- Psychology practical sessions.
- Peer coaching workshops.
- Training sessions for active listening and empathy.

## Roles
1. **Coach**: Facilitates the conversation using open-ended questions (e.g., GROW model). Focuses on listening and reflecting.
2. **Coachee**: Shares a real or simulated challenge/goal.
3. **Observer**: Does not participate in the conversation. Takes notes on the Coach's performance, specifically looking for:
    - Active listening indicators (nodding, paraphrasing).
    - Use of open vs. closed questions.
    - Instances of "advice-giving" (to be avoided).

## Steps
1. **Setup**: Divide participants into groups of three. Assign roles.
2. **Coaching Session (10-15 mins)**: Coach and Coachee engage. Observer takes notes.
3. **Feedback Loop (5-10 mins)**:
    - **Coachee** shares how they felt (was I heard? did I find my own solution?).
    - **Observer** provides objective data on the Coach's techniques.
    - **Coach** reflects on their own performance.
4. **Rotation**: Swap roles until everyone has played each role.

## Output Format
If documenting a session, use:
- **Participants**: [Names/Roles]
- **Key Challenge**: [Brief description]
- **Observer Notes**: [Bullet points on listening/questioning]
- **Action Items**: [Coachee's self-derived next steps]

## Example
> **Observer Note**: "Coach used paraphrasing at the 5-minute mark which helped Coachee clarify their priority. However, at 8 minutes, Coach suggested a specific solution ('Why don't you try X?'), which shifted the dynamic from coaching to advising."
