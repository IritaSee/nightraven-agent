# FGD Funneling Technique

Use this skill to structure Focus Group Discussion (FGD) guides that build rapport and context before diving into sensitive or specific research objectives.

## When to Use
- Qualitative research design.
- Community feedback sessions.
- Product or service evaluation.

## The Funnel Structure
1. **Opening (Wide)**: General, non-threatening questions about the topic area to make participants comfortable.
2. **Transition (Narrowing)**: Questions that connect general experiences to the specific research focus.
3. **Key Questions (Specific)**: The core objectives of the study. Detailed and probing.
4. **Closing (Wrap-up)**: Summary and "anything else?" questions.

## Steps
1. **Define Objective**: What is the single most important thing you need to learn?
2. **Draft General Questions**: Start with "How do you usually..." or "What comes to mind when you hear..."
3. **Bridge to Specifics**: Use "Thinking about [Topic], what has been your experience with [Specific Aspect]?"
4. **Refine Probes**: Prepare follow-up questions for the "Key" section (e.g., "Can you give an example?").

## Output Format
- **FGD Topic**: [Title]
- **Phase 1 (General)**: [2-3 Questions]
- **Phase 2 (Transition)**: [1-2 Questions]
- **Phase 3 (Specific)**: [3-5 Questions]
- **Phase 4 (Closing)**: [1-2 Questions]

## Example
> **Topic: Use of Digital Health Apps**
> - **General**: "How do you usually manage your health information?"
> - **Transition**: "When you search for health info online, what platforms do you use?"
> - **Specific**: "What specific features in the SakuMedis app do you find confusing? How does the STR verification process affect your trust?"
> - **Closing**: "If you could change one thing about the app, what would it be?"
